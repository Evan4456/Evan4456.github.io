Goals

For this work term I had four goals: three technical and one general. The three technical ones were to develop my python scripting skills, improve my proficiency with SQL and to learn the necessary technologies that I would need to create my intranet page. The final, non tech related, skill was to enhance my ability to communicate my ideas, document my work and teach others how to use my technology. To begin with my non-technical learning goal, improving my oral communication. The way in which I did this was by running training sessions to teach my peers how to use some of the technology that I worked on. This was a goal for me since I have never done an instructional presentation before, let alone on technology. There were also certain challenges I found in addition to the novelty of the task like how to cater to people with different technological skill levels as well as people who do not primarily speak English. Due to the varying skill levels of the people in my audience, from people in technical roles who are very tech savvy to mine administration personnel who have far less technical experience, learning how to speak in a way that is clear for everyone, even if I know I am dumbing it down more than some people need was quite difficult. I found myself feeling so worried about sounding condescending to those that did know what they were talking about, that I may not have gone enough in depth for those who did not. Additionally, the fact that my audience was Finnish lead to me producing strong visual aids along with my oral presentation so that the strange technical names for buttons that my audience my not have known would be able to translate over to Finnish more intuitively. Overall, I think I did make good progress on completing this although there were some unanticipated challenges as I mentioned, and I believe my next step is to work on presenting in a way that is accessible to all skill levels.
